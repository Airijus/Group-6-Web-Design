Laws Of Indices

Laws Of Indices GCSE Maths, You will learn what the laws of indices are and how to apply them in mathematical problems. In this lesson you will learn how to Multiply indices, Divide indices, use brackets and how to raise to the power of 0 and the power of 1 and lastly fractional and negative indices

What Are Laws of Indices?

Indices are rules of simplifying calculations and expressions that involve powers of the same base. This means that a larger number or letter must be the same.

Example : 23 x 27 = 210

Example : x10 ÷ x4 = x6

We cannot use laws of indices to evaluate calculations when the bases are different

Example : 25 x 33

Example : x10 ÷ y4

What Are The 6 Laws Of Indices?

The 6 laws of indicees are:

Multiplying indices, Dividing indicies, Brackets with indices, Power of 0, Negative indices, Fractional indices
