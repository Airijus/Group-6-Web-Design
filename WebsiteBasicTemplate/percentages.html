What is a percentage?

A percentage is a fraction that is parts out of 100. It is indicated by the % symbol, and can be an integer (24%), mixed number (511⁄2%), fraction (2⁄5%), or a decimal (9.4%).

It can also have any valve meaning it can go above 100% (175%), or be a smaller number (0.2%).


For example, 25 out of 100 (or 25⁄100) is 25% and is the same as a quarter (1⁄4).

This is easier than it seems.

  1. 1. Add all given percentages.
    1. (e.g.) 12% + 25% = 37%
  2. 2. Subtract the result from 100%.
    1. (e.g.) 100% - 37% = 63%
  3. 3. The difference between 100% and the total given percentage will be the missing percentage.

Remember that the whole is always 100% unless the question tells you otherwise.

Percentage as an operator

A percentage becomes an operator when it represents a mathematical action.


For example, 20% of 100 means work out 20 hundredths of 100.

(e.g.) 20⁄100 x 100 = 20

Using combinations of percentages to find a percentage

This method uses set percentages like 1%, 10%, and 50%, as building blocks to help.


[1% CONVERTS] 2%, 3%, and 4% can be found by multiply 1% by 2, 3, and 4 respectively.


[10% CONVERTS] 5% is half (÷2) of 10%. 20% is double (x2) of 10%.


[50% CONVERTS] 25% is half (÷2) of 50%. 75% is triple (x3) of 25%.


Example: What is 46% of 150?


46% = 10% + 10% + 10% + 10% + 5% + 1%

Same as:

46% = 10% x 4 + 5% + 1%


[10%] 10% of 150 is 15.

[5%] 5% is half 10%. 15 ÷ 2 = 7.5.

[1%] 1% of 150 is 1.5.

So 46% of 150 = 15 x 4 + 7.5 + 1.5 = 69
