In this lesson, we will go over what trigonometry is at GCSE standards.

SohCahToa: The fundamental saying for trigonometry

trigonometry is all about triangles, and angles inside of them.

if you have a right angle triangle, and you know one angle, and the lengths of two sides, you can figure out the other angles inside.

using this, we are able to work out the angle inside the triangle. now you may be wondering, "how can we do this?" and that is where SohCahToa comes in!


SohCahToa is a term used to remember to use Sine, Cosine, and Tangent. to use this, we need to know what the Opposite, Adjacent, and Hypotenuse lengths of a triangle are. the easiest way to remember is this:

The Opposite length is "opposite" the Unknown angle

The Adjacent length is inbetween the right angle and unknown angle

The Hypotenuse is oppsite to the right angle


going back to our example, we know what the opposite and adjacent sides are, meaning we have the O and the A.
now looking at SohCahToa, we can split it into Soh, Cah, and Toa. the O and A are both in "Toa", meaning we use tan! therefore, the angle is Tan(?)=O/A, which equals Tan(?) = 3/6

to therefore figure out the angle, we can use the inverse tan function to get ? = tan^-1(3/6), which equals 26.57 degrees to 2 d.p!
